QUsable

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 29 March 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how QUsable ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects information when you use our service at qusable.com.

QUsable is operated by a sole trader based in the United Kingdom. We are registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as a data controller.

1. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to two types of user:

  • Assessors - Dyslexia Diagnosticians and similar professionals who create an account to manage learner records.
  • Learners - individuals completing a background questionnaire using an access code provided by their assessor.

2. What data we collect

Assessors

  • Account data: email address and password (stored securely via Supabase Auth).
  • Learner records: names, access codes, and questionnaire responses entered by assessors. These are encrypted with a key derived from your password before being stored.
  • Subscription data: billing information is handled entirely by Stripe. We store a Stripe customer ID and your subscription status only.
  • Recovery key: an encrypted copy of your encryption key is stored server-side to enable account recovery. See the Recovery Key Policy for full details.

Learners

  • Questionnaire responses: stored locally in your browser (IndexedDB), encrypted with a key derived from your access code. No learner data is sent to our servers unless you choose to submit your responses to your assessor.
  • We do not collect learner names, email addresses, or any other identifying information server-side.

3. How we use your data

  • To provide and maintain the QUsable service.
  • To authenticate assessors and protect access to their data.
  • To process subscription payments and manage account access.
  • To enable encrypted cloud sync of assessor data across devices.
  • To enable account recovery at the assessor's verified request.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose beyond operating the service.

4. Legal basis for processing (UK GDPR)

  • Contract performance: processing necessary to provide the service you have subscribed to.
  • Legitimate interests: security, fraud prevention, service improvement.
  • Legal obligation: compliance with applicable UK law.

Learner questionnaire responses are processed by assessors under their own professional obligations. Assessors are responsible for ensuring they have an appropriate lawful basis for collecting and processing learner data.

5. Data storage and security

  • All assessor learner records are encrypted client-side using AES-GCM (256-bit) with keys derived via PBKDF2 (600,000 iterations) before being transmitted or stored.
  • Learner responses are stored only in the browser's local storage unless the learner submits them to their assessor.
  • Encrypted data is stored on servers operated by Netlify, Inc. in the United States. Netlify is covered by the UK-US Data Bridge.
  • Authentication is managed by Supabase, Inc. Data is stored in EU-West (Ireland) region.

6. Data processors

We use the following third-party processors:

  • Supabase - authentication and encryption key storage. Privacy Policy
  • Netlify - hosting and encrypted data storage. Privacy Policy
  • Stripe - payment processing. Privacy Policy
  • Resend - transactional email (account confirmations, password resets). Privacy Policy
  • Anthropic - AI key notes generation. When an assessor generates key notes for a custom questionnaire, the questionnaire structure (question text and types) is sent to Anthropic's API. Learner responses are never sent to Anthropic. Privacy Policy

Each processor is bound by a Data Processing Agreement and we only share the minimum data necessary for them to perform their service.

7. Data retention

  • Assessor account data is retained while your account is active and for up to 12 months after closure.
  • Encrypted learner records stored in cloud sync are retained while your account is active. You may delete individual records at any time.
  • Learner data stored locally in the browser is retained until the learner clears their browser storage.
  • Submitted learner responses stored on our servers are deleted when the assessor deletes the learner record, or automatically after 180 days, whichever is sooner.
  • Stripe retains billing records as required by law.

8. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate data.
  • Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Restrict or object to processing.
  • Data portability.
  • Withdraw consent at any time (where processing is based on consent).

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@qusable.com. We will respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

9. Cookies

QUsable does not use tracking or advertising cookies. We use only technically necessary browser storage (localStorage and IndexedDB) to operate the application.

10. Children's data

Learners may be under 18. Learner responses are collected by assessors as part of a professional dyslexia assessment process. Assessors are responsible for obtaining appropriate parental or guardian consent where required, in accordance with their professional obligations.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated to assessors by email. The current version is always available at qusable.com/privacy.html.
